Why I stopped using Cloudflare

Date: Feb-10-2021

First off... I have decided to stop using Cloudflare and immediately remove from all of my websites... Cloudflare is a service used by websites to mitigate DDOS attacks, provide free SSL certificates, provide free storage on a Static CDN among other things. Their entire business strategy is to be the middle-man, they are in the middle between your browser and the server checking if you are a bot, or for anything else...

That poses a huge privacy risk to my readers, and I don't even need DDOS mitigation or free SSL certificates or a static CDN. The only reason I chose Cloudflare was because it had a huge amount of DNS record types, My domain registrar only has 6-9 types while cloudflare has 11-12 or maybe even more, and also I used it because it had a simple user interface, that was the only reason I used it.

Cloudflare has an option to turn off Cloudflare's proxy (So that Cloudflare isn't between your browser and the server) for DNS records, I turned them off because I was running an email server and I didn't want it to break. but I also didn't want to look like I was supporting Cloudflare, I didn't want to endorse them and I definitely did not want them to gain more websites.

Cloudflare discriminates against Tor and VPN users. I don't care if Tor or VPN users enter my website, but Cloudflare does, and Cloudflare sends a captcha (A proprietary captcha that likely has trackers inside) and The captcha disappears for the rest of the browser session which means that Cloudflare sets a cookie that uniquely identifies the Tor/VPN user.

This is unacceptable to me, I believe in privacy and what Cloudflare is doing is just disgusting... Then there are also security bugs, in 2017, a security vulnerability called Cloudbleed was discovered... it allowed attackers to see private things such as HTTP POST Data or Session keys...

then there is also censorship, Cloudflare terminated 8chan's website, This meant that 8chan was vulnerable to DDOS attacks... I think 8chan deserved it because it was a right-wing propaganda hotspot and they were associated with several right-wing mass shootings (including the New Zealnd mosque attacks, which hurt me on a personal level because I am a muslim) but this is still alarming, Cloudflare could terminate your service if they don't like what you are doing.

These 3 reasons among others made me think about whether or not I really needed Cloudflare, and I changed my domain's nameservers to my domain registrar's nameservers and copied the DNS records. Now I don't need Cloudflare, which is good for me. Why should I bother with a third-party company when my domain registrar can offer the same services while respecting my readers privacy?

If you use Cloudflare then I want you to really think about whether or not you need it, think about the security bugs, terms and conditions and Proxy discrimination. I really hope you can leave Cloudflare but if you are a huge website susceptible to DDOS attacks then Its okay, I get it. but try exploring other options too.
